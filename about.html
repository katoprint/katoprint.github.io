Artist Biography

    Caden Sato handles digital prints with archival pigment on a mix of watercolor and archival papers. Caden makes use of a full bleed technique with digital prints, breaking the borders of conventional archival prints. Further pushing this theme, large popouts that resemble those of children's books adds a layer of excitement to an otherwise 2D space. His work often emulates the style and technique of manga and comic books, representing narratives through a sequence of panels meant to lead the viewer through small sections of a larger story. Brutalist/form follows function style architecture are common settings for these stories. Caden often conflates these two separate themes to create over the top and quirky narratives that challenge identity and professionalism. Caden is currently studying at the University of Hawaii at Manoa. He is set to graduate in 2027 with a Bachelors of Fine Arts. For the past three years he has been studying digital imaging under Scott Groeniger, building his knowledge for handling professional as well as experimental ink jet printing. In 2025, Caden received the most outstanding undergraduate student in digital imaging from the University of Hawaii at Manoa.
